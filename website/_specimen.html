The site is an editor, not a brochure. A numbered gutter, an asymmetric grid, one face with a voice and one that gets out of the way.

Nothing here is marketing copy. This page exists so the system can be judged before a single sentence is written on top of it, and so a regression can be seen.

02
Prose · Inter

The body face gets out of the way

This paragraph is set in Inter at 16px on a 1.65 leading, capped at 62 characters. It is a neutral grotesk on purpose: the personality of this site lives in the display face, the grid, and the two colours. A body face with opinions would fight all three.

This block is right-anchored — columns 6 through 12. The section above it is left-anchored. Alternating the two is the rhythm of the page. Nothing is centred except the mark in the hero, and that is the point: a centred column is what every terminal-flavoured site does.

03
Colour · two states

Ember is input. Signal is output.

A terminal has exactly two things in it: what you typed, and what came back. Ember marks the prompt, the accent, the one call to action per screen. Signal marks a success the product actually earned. Neither is ever used to decorate.
